SA Kenya is launching **Support for Institutions and Professionals (SIP)** as a clearer public pathway for outreach to therapists, clergy, counselors, rehab programmes, and institutions that may encounter those who still suffer.

The goal is simple: make it easier for referral partners to understand what Sexaholics Anonymous is, what it is not, and how to connect interested people to recovery support.

This new site structure supports that goal in several ways:

- a general SIP landing page
- a page specifically for professionals
- a page specifically for clergy
- a locally accessible pamphlet library
- a blog space for event updates, literature highlights, and follow-up communication

Current immediate priorities include:

- building contact lists for outreach
- strengthening email-based communication
- preparing for a professional awareness event
- creating clean follow-up paths for people who want to learn more

If you are a professional, institution, or ministry leader who would like to receive a briefing or literature pack, contact SA Kenya through the contact links provided on this website.
